What I Bring To The Table

I'm a technical marketing manager. I've worked in most channels within marketing, and I'm proficient in every discipline that makes up the team: design, SEO, paid media, content writing, email marketing.

That shows up in practice, not on a CV. When design is short-handed, I generate the assets myself, in line with the brand. When our paid media manager is out, I manage the ads. I run content writing directly. There is no seat on this team I haven't sat in.

I've done every job on this team, so I know what good looks like, what it costs, and how long it should take. That's how a tight ship runs.

It's also why the team stays small and the output doesn't. I vibe coded the Telegram affiliate bot the affiliate base now uses daily. Strong results from a lean team isn't an accident, it's the operating model.

And it doesn't stop at marketing's borders. From next week, as planned, Bruno starts affiliate outreach inside the marketing team, feeding affiliate managers a steady stream of leads. Why? Because he has more time to spare and more to give, and I'm aware of where time goes and how long tasks really take. Empowering the team to do more is part of the job.

To be clear, none of this is a claim that I can do everything and don't need a team. I need this team, and they deliver. It's a claim about dedication: when a gap opens, I'm the one putting in the late hours to make it happen.
